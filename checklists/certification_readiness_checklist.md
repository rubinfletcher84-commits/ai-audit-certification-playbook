# ISO/IEC 42001 Certification Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist to assess whether an organization is ready to pursue AI management system certification readiness activities.

## Objective
Confirm that the organization has enough governance structure, operational evidence, and corrective-action discipline to support an ISO/IEC 42001-style readiness review.

## Control checks
- [ ] Scope of the AI management system is defined and approved
- [ ] Accountable management representative or program owner is named
- [ ] AI governance policies are published and in use
- [ ] AI inventory is complete enough to support audit sampling
- [ ] High-impact AI use cases are identified and classified
- [ ] Risk assessments are performed before launch or major change
- [ ] Impact assessments are performed for material AI use cases
- [ ] Human oversight is defined for important decisions
- [ ] AI-related incident response procedures exist
- [ ] Monitoring and review evidence exists for deployed AI systems
- [ ] Internal audit or control review has been performed
- [ ] Management review has been held
- [ ] Corrective actions are tracked to closure
- [ ] Major gaps between policy and operational practice are being addressed

## Sample evidence
- Scope statement
- Governance charter
- AI policy set
- AI inventory
- Risk and impact assessment records
- Incident response playbooks
- Monitoring reports
- Internal audit records
- Management review minutes
- Corrective action tracker

## Red flags
- No defined AIMS scope
- No accountable owner
- No usable AI inventory
- No risk or impact review for important use cases
- No user training or no evidence of policy awareness
- No incident path for harmful output, leakage, or unsafe automation
- Major mismatch between written policy and actual practice
