# AI Vendor Assurance Checklist

Use this checklist to assess third-party AI providers, model vendors, SaaS copilots, API providers, and embedded AI products.

## Objective
Verify that external AI providers are reviewed, contracted, monitored, and governed with the same seriousness as internal AI systems.

## Control checks
- [ ] Vendor due diligence is completed before onboarding
- [ ] Security, privacy, legal, and business stakeholders review high-risk AI vendors
- [ ] Contracts address customer data use, retention, and model training rights
- [ ] Contracts define incident notification expectations
- [ ] Vendor documentation explains model limitations and supported use cases
- [ ] Subprocessors or downstream providers are disclosed where relevant
- [ ] Material vendor changes trigger reassessment
- [ ] Offboarding or exit planning exists for critical vendors
- [ ] High-risk vendors have contingency plans
- [ ] The organization can identify which business units use each AI vendor

## Sample evidence
- Vendor due diligence questionnaires
- Contracts and DPAs
- Security review records
- Privacy review memos
- Model cards or vendor documentation
- Change notifications
- Exit or contingency planning documents

## Red flags
- A vendor was adopted by one team with no formal review
- The contract is silent on customer data use for training or model improvement
- No one can explain vendor retention behavior
- High-risk vendors have no backup plan
- Vendor changes are not monitored after onboarding
