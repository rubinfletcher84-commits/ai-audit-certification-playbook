# AI Governance and Accountability Checklist

Use this checklist to verify that AI is governed as a managed organizational program with clear ownership, policy direction, and oversight.

## Objective
Confirm that the organization has executive sponsorship, defined accountability, approved policies, and a repeatable oversight process for AI use.

## Control checks
- [ ] A named executive or accountable owner is assigned for AI governance
- [ ] AI governance roles and responsibilities are documented
- [ ] An AI governance committee or oversight function exists
- [ ] AI policies cover internal development, procurement, deployment, and use of third-party AI tools
- [ ] There is a formal approval path for new AI use cases
- [ ] Material changes to AI systems require review and approval
- [ ] Internal audit or control review processes include AI
- [ ] Corrective actions are tracked and assigned
- [ ] Management review is performed on a defined cadence
- [ ] Business units are not using AI independently without central governance rules

## Sample evidence
- AI governance charter
- Policy documents
- Organization chart
- Committee meeting minutes
- Approval records
- Internal audit records
- Corrective action tracker

## Red flags
- No single accountable AI owner
- AI policy exists but is not enforced
- Different business units follow different AI rules
- No evidence of governance meetings or reviews
- No escalation path for governance issues
